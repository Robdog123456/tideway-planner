HOW TO READ IT
ROW — tide and wind both green. Ideal.
CAUTION — amber somewhere, nothing red. Row with care.
DON'T ROW — anything red closes the window.
NO LAUNCH — embankment flooded at HW. Launching and landing blocked; being afloat is fine.
TIDE ONLY — beyond the wind forecast (~14 days). Tide windows shown, no verdicts faked.
HOW FAR CAN I GO — SESSIONS
The v2 model rows the whole out-and-back from Putney through six reaches, per turn point (Hammersmith → Kew), for a launch at that slot. A turn is green only if every reach stays green for the full round trip. BEST TURN is the furthest still rowable in ≤ 135 min.
HW GATE — EMBANKMENT FLOODING
Putney HW ≥ 5.90 m — AMBER: parts of the Embankment road may flood around high water. Time the launch; expect wet feet. ≥ 6.30 m — RED: above anything boated — no launching or landing through HW ("NO LAUNCH"). Being afloat through high water is fine.
TIDE LIGHT — THE BLACK-FLAG RULE
Do not boat at low tide. Red ≈ 1 h before to 2 h after low water. Amber = the flanking half-hours. Green = the rest of the ebb and the risen flood.
WIND LIGHT — SUSTAINED SPEED
Green ≤ 8 mph · Amber 9–13 · Red > 13 mph, or gusts ≥ 32, or wind-over-tide once already breezy.
EBB-TIDE FLAG — HOW IT'S SET
At 06:00 and 18:00 the lowest Richmond (EA 0009) reading of the preceding 12 h sets the flag: ≥ 2.6 m RED · 1.7–2.6 YELLOW · 0–1.7 GREEN · < 0 BLACK. The app computes it live from EA readings when online, else shows the pipeline snapshot. Display-only — it never changes a verdict. Cross-check at pla.co.uk.
SOURCES
Calibrated per-reach model v2, rebuilt every 6 h on the pipeline. PLA tide predictions; wind to ~14 days ({{windEnd}}); 40-member ensemble confidence to ~7 days. Rowing hours 04:30–20:30; pre-sunrise slots noted as dark. The grid JSON is the single source of truth — the app renders it and adds nothing.
NOT MODELLED
Fluvial surges beyond the flag snapshot, commercial traffic and debris. This app informs — it never authorises. The club / PLA flag on the day always governs.
