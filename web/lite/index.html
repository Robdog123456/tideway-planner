TIDE LIGHT — THE BLACK-FLAG BAND

RED from LW−1h to LW+2h, AMBER flanks to −2h/+2:30. This is a club rule (LRC black-flag policy), not physics — I've been afloat at 0.67 m Putney. The band stays because the rule stays.

WIND LIGHT — SUSTAINED SPEED

GREEN ≤ 8 mph · AMBER 9–13 · RED > 13, sustained, not gusts — gusts are info-only until 32 mph. Wind-over-tide escalates amber to red per reach when the wind opposes the local stream (±50°) at ≥ 13 mph sustained.

SESSIONS — THE V2 MODEL

The boat is simulated out-and-back through six reaches whose axes swing 338°→231°; conditions are scored where the boat actually is, when it's there. Stream fitted from my own GPS: flood +0.6–1.0 m/s, early ebb −0.9–1.4, late ebb −0.6–0.8. Backtested 29/29 real sessions.

EMBANKMENT GATE — TWO TIERS (LAUNCH/LANDING ONLY)

AMBER ≥ 5.90 m Putney: parts of the road may flood around HW on big springs — time the launch, wet feet possible. RED ≥ 6.30 m: above anything I've ever boated (max 6.25 m). Rowing through HW afloat is unaffected.

THE FLAG — COMPUTED, NOT JUDGED

At 06:00/18:00 the lowest Richmond reading of the preceding 12 h sets it: ≥ 2.6 m RED · 1.7–2.6 YELLOW · 0–1.7 GREEN · < 0 BLACK.

BLACK — don't boat at low tide. RED — CRSA clearance, low flood only. GREEN — caution and good lookout.

Cross-check on the day: pla.co.uk/ebb-tide-flag-warning

HONESTY LIMITS — NOT MODELLED

Tides are knowable ~a month out; wind is not. Beyond ~14 days the grid is tide-only — no verdicts. Ensemble confidence reaches ~7 days. Chop, bridge-arch gust acceleration and springs/neaps stream scaling are noted in the Code but not modelled. Wind is model output at 10 m, not river level.
